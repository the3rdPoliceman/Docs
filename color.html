B: Color
Basic Rules
R: Start with black & white
Initial design work is best done in black and white - if it doesn't work in black and white, it won't work in color
R: Then add ONE color
Having a single "Primary" color will help create a more cohesive design scheme - using loads of different colors will make it difficult to have consistency, and also difficult to make things stand out if you want to use accents or "calls to action" later. Choose your starting color by
  • Matching the brand
  • If you selected photos already, match with them
  • Pick one you like
R: Play with variations of your one color
Using different shades of your one color is a good way of distinguishing different elements. For example, if you chose a dark blue as a primary color, you might use lighter or darker versions for headers, links, names...whatever...
R: Think carefully before adding another primary color
Having another secondary color is fine. Avoid colors which don't go well together. Online color choosers or adobe colors will help find complmentary colors, which will work well. Complmentary colors will help bring attention to information, headings, buttons etc.
R: Get rid of the black and white
Absolute blacks and whites are often a bit too harsh. For the final design, it's often good to replace black with dark grey, and white with very light grey
