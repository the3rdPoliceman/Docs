Powder Coating Metal
Powder Coating Metal
Powder coating is an excellent method for coating metal surfaces, because it
  • Provides good protection
  • No clean up, at electrostatics attract powder to the metal
  • Relatively odor free during application and baking
  • Only low pressure is needed in the paint gun
Prepare surface
The surface should be prepared by one of these methods, or sandblasting AND heating. (details here)
  • Blueing - heat to 300C (can't go much over), by oven or torch. This also helps get rid of trapped moisture which could affect the coating process
  • Sand blasting
  • Using a bathroom powdered clearer, like ajax or comet
Masking
Masking before applying primers, powders and finishes is essential. It can be done with
  • High-temperature silicone plugs
  • Kapton tape
Fixing defects before painting
Small defects can be fixed by filling with the paint. The part is heated, powder is sprinkled on and will fill defects, and it can then be sanded before final powder coating. (details here)

Application
The part is grounded, and the paint electrostatically charged as it comes out of the gun, so it is attracted to the part (details here and here)
Getting to inside corners can require lower voltages, so it can be a good idea to spray inside corners at lower voltage, then up the voltage and complete the job

Baking
Baking is done around 230C.

Rough details can be found on Dan Gelbarts video where he recommends
  • 5 minutes at 235C
  • 190C for 15 minutes
Probably this is good enough for basic coating, but not professional
