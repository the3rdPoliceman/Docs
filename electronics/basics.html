General Info
There are 2 types of DC power supply - Linear vs Switching DC Power Supplies - What's the Difference? https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v_HtdbsV69k

Switching seems most common, but not sure which is generally used for motors. Pros and cons are
  • Switching supplies are more efficient
  • Switching supplies are smaller
  • Linear supplies provide a much smoother supply (less noise)
  • Linear supplies have a much faster response

Relays, Solenoids & Contactors
When would you use a contactor rather than a relay? If you're using something that needs a lot of power e.g. above 240V 5A you'd normally use a contactor. Probably should learn more before deciding that though
Choosing a relay. Recommended Relays 4RD 931 410-05 or similar (available on aliexpress). Recommended by devin vanderhoof for car stuff. Reason for recommendation - skirted, watertight, nice terminator setup
Relays
  • Eletromechanical Relays - use an electromagnetic coil to attract a movable contact. Often have multiple sets of contacts, so one input change can utrn on/off multiple decvices
  • Solid State Relays - Can activate with very little power. Mo movable parts = longer life than electromechanical. Switch faster. Can deal with higher power than EM. Less issues with arcing

Inductors
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ukBFPrXiKWA gives an explanation in simple terms Considerations when using
  • Pay attention to the maximum current allowed through an inductor. They are limited by resistance and dissipate heat, so need to be chosen appropriately
  • Inductors tend to be much larger, physically, than capacitors are for storing equivalent amounts of energy
  • The magnetic fields from inductors can affect nearby components on a circuit board. These fields can extend significant distances beyond the inductor. This is especially true if there are other inductors nearby. They can link up, and fuck up
  • After current to the inductor has shut off, the current generated by the inductor needs some well thought out place to dissipate. Otherwise it will find a place itself. The voltage produced by the inductor as the current to the inductor is shuts off will be very high - much higher than the supply voltage, and enough to create a spark and generally fuck things up
Transistors
The three main types are
  • BJTs: bipolar junction transistors
  • FETs: field-effect transistors, including the most common, MOSFETs
  • IGBTs: insulated-gate bipolar transistors
