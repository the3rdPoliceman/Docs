Printing Stronger

AFFECT OF FAN SETTING ON LAYER ADHESION
PLA - The lower the fan setting, the stronger the adhesion
PETG - The lower the fan setting, the stronger the adhesion
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Bl2ESvtBiLo

AFFECT OF EXTRUSION WIDTH ON LAYER ADHESION
Wider extrusions increases layer adhesion and therefore strength by up to around 50%. There are limits to how far you can push this, after which parts get ugly - around 150% extrusion width seems to be about the maximum
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9YaJ0wSKKHA

AFFECT OF INFILL PERCENT AND SHELL STRENGTH
You'll get beter bang for your buck by increasing shell count/thickness rather than infill percentage. For max strength, 5 perimeters and 50% infill.
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AmEaNAwFSfI

AFFECT OF INFILL PATTERN AND SHELL STRENGTH
Gyroid or cubic for parts that need to be strong in all directions. For parts that need strength in one axis, rectilinear, grid and triangle are superior to gyroid and cubic, and all give similar results
https://youtu.be/upELI0HmzHc?t=398

AFFECT OF INFILL PATTERN ON STRENGTH
Honeycomb gives best strength to weight, closely followed by triangular. Triangular actually gives stonger parts for the same infill percentage, but that is because it actually infills more than the specified amount
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AmEaNAwFSfI

Generating Easily Removable Support
  • Use the lines infill pattern
  • Try as low as 5% infill. seems to almost always work without problems
  • Use clippers for support removal & a soldering iron ferrule for smoothing
Storing Filament

In general for all plastic filament, it should be stored in as dry conditions as possible. If water gets into the filament it can cause all sorts of issues. This isn't such a big deal with PLA (although it does increase stringing etc.), but can make a huge difference for other filaments.

Ideally you should store filament in sealed boxes with silica dessicant. If having trouble with trickier filaments like TPU or nylon, its worth exploring drying them in the oven. See https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqQRN9TUw08 for a guide

Threaded Metal Inserts for 3D prints
Based on this video from CNC kitchen
  • Best Overall: Heat set inserts A threaded insert that can be heated and pushed into the 3D print with a soldering iron (example here) . Ruthex style with opposing diagonal grooves on the outside are really good. Not far behind hex nuts for strength, and good enough for most things
  • Best for Strength: Hex nuts on opposite side Best for strength, but a bit more annoying to fit and they have a tendency to fall out
  • Helicoils - wtf are helicoils? Thingies that look a bit like springs. They're for fixing spripped out threads in a recess where replacement isn't an option e.g. bike frame. You drill out the hole nicely, tap it, screw in the helicoil, and then the inside of the helicoil acts as the replacement thread. Not great for 3D prints, they don't really improve over screwing directly into the plastic. But cool idea
Threaded Plastic Parts Guide
Based on this video from Product Design Online
When designing
  • You'll need more clearance for plastic threads. When designing threads, add more clearance than suggested by default by offsetting thread faces (0.2mm total offset is a good estimate). Slice through threads with a section to inspect the clearance
  • Finer pitches won't normally work very well in plastic
  • Plastic threads with sharp edges will degrade quickly. Round the edge of threads & grooves with fillets (start from 0.15mm fillet)
Printing options
  • Reducing layer height when slicing will help. Maybe go to 0.15 or less
  • Increasing the temperature 15C to improve layer adhesion
  • For printing most things, I use "print outer before inner". Maybe this is a bad idea with threaded parts?
  • Should also try "print infill before walls"
Gears
When designing
  • Don't let the plastic get too thin around the bearing i.e. bearing diameter should probably not be much more than half gear diameter.
  • Tensioners wih toothed belt drive help avoid slipping a lot
Printing options
  • Where elephants foot can be an issue i.e. when gear teeth will be directly on the print surface, use a raft
In Use
  • Lubrication - Have seen someone using carlube multipurpose silicon grease
