LEARNING PROGRESSION - FIRST TIME TO LINKING TURNS
Intro to equipment: Board, Bindings, How to strap in Binding Details: Toe strap Heel Strap Highback Trying on: Find a flat spot to try board on Strap in front foot. Heel strap first, to seat foot down and back, then toe strap. One foot in: Introduce toe edge, heel edge by Step behind board with free foot - now heel edge is on the snow Step over the board with the loose foot - now toe edge is on the snow Intraduce other Board details: Nose Tail Play around with one foot in: Slide it left and right. Step over the board and back. Pick up the board completely while on the free foot See what difference angling the base makes regarding how the edge bites/butters Walk up hill with one foot in - Step, board, step, board Skating: Try skating on flat. Decide if you like skating with your foot behind the board or with your foot in front. Key points: Stand up! Look where you're going, not at your feet Skating 2: Sliding with 1 foot in Check: Posture - should be standing up, stacked Skating 3 - Advanced Messing: Little jump while skating ( on flat/nearly flat) Scooch along Heel side one foot J-turn: Analogy: ski poles jammed in boots to steer (details of how levers are used to go toe edge, heel edge, turn https://youtu.be/lpx2kH96L_A?t=297) Front foot lever is most important for turning https://youtu.be/lpx2kH96L_A?t=368 Keeping weight more on the front foot, by pushing the front knee towards the tip over the ankle and bring it around You can hang the heel of your back foot over the edge of the board to act as an emergency brake if you want Common Error: No tension at all in body - boarder tries to turn starting with the upper body, which can work, but nothing happens. You need some tension in core - a spaghetti body doesn't pass the message through to the legs and hips Helping Hand: You can take the learners front hand to gently pull them along and into the turn Toe side J-turn: Common Error: Bending over as turn finishes on toe edge. You end up looking down at the snow, bent over, ass & hips go back which flattens the board Toe Edge Side Slip: Pre Exercise: Practice posture without board again. Gently bounce up and down to check flexing Mention: Standard good posture applies. Knees and ankles flexed. Hips forward. Spine pointing to toe edge in this case. IDEA: Can one student correct another to reinforce learning? First time: Get learner up, hold hand, check posture - head up, hips forward, flexed knees and ankles. Warning: Hips forward to correct back edge getting close to catching When sliding, try to relax Feel: Shins push in the front of the boots Both levers are pushed forward Common Error: Tensing calf muscles and toe standing to raise back edge and get toe edge grip, rather than using knee flex and hip forward (using calves is very tiring and cramping is likely) Note: Called toe edge for obvious reason, but really you're on the balls of your feet Remind Them: This is the most difficult (but least scary) slope to learn this on Toe Edge Traverse: What we're doing: Pulling levers towards nose, tail of board (not twisting yet, that comes with falling leaf) Pre Exercise: Practice posture without board again. Practice avtivating levers (moving lead leg into position) Common Problem: Boarder doesn't really traverse. They need to do a more exaggerated lead leg motion - rehearse it, or prompt them what to feel Toe Edge Falling Leaf: What we're doing: Pulling levers out AND AROUND towards nose, tail of board to initiate downhill motion, then back to neutral to slow to a stop. Both directions Explanation: Describe the pattern we're looking for. Maybe draw it in the snow Pre Exercise: Practice posture without board again. Practice activating levers - out and around Analogy: out and around - like opening a door Feeling: The weight shifting from the balls of the feet, onto the outside edge of your foot (as if you're trying to twist it out of the binding). TODO: Should Investigate the feeling myself in the boots, foot soles, legs Common Error: Pushing the leading heel down, rather than pulling the knee out and around Common Error: Too much weight on back foot, rather than committing, due to fear Heel Edge Side Slip: What we're doing: Pulling levers out AND AROUND towards nose, tail of board to initiate downhill motion, then back to neutral to slow to a stop. Both directions Pre Exercise: Practice posture without board again (maybe with support of a wall or person) - like you're gonna sit down on a chair. Both levers are back in the default heel edge posture. Practice avtivating levers (moving lead leg into position) Heel Edge Traverse: As toe edge. Push levers toward nose or tail of board to move in that direction. Return to neutral to stop sideways movement Heel Edge Falling Leaf: As toe edge, but instead of swinging levers around like a door, the lever goes forward in front of the body and towards the nose/tail. Foot rolls onto outside Common error: Bending over forwards. Out of balance and toes have to lift up board. Common error: Legs too straight. Harder to control speed, and levers aren't as effective Heel to toe Turns: Joins a movements together that have already been learned - Heel edge Side slip Push front knee out to side, to start sliding down diagonally like traverse Front lever forward to turn it down the slope (like falling leaf) Back lever follows when finishing up the turn Helping hand: Instructor can lead the learner with one hand during the exercise (like a fancy lady). Is also useful as it keeps the hand out in front, moving weight forward. Stand up a little in the turn like this to ease the transition between edges
